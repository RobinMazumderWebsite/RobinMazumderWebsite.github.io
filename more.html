India Trip
Summer 2013
As my younger sister and I grow as kids, there is less and less opportunity to visit the mother country. This trip was well timed for two reasons, (1) I had just finished my high school education and (2) I hadn't been to India 6 years prior and I probably won't go again in another 6 years. I'm born and raised in Canada and am very much Canadian, so though I understand the culture well and the language a little bit, India is still very foreign to me. Because of this, my trips to India always end up being very educational - there always seems to be something to learn.

On this trip I had to learn vulnerability. I really don't have much independence in India; the locals can quickly sniff out foreigners and manipulate them for their own gain. If that sounds scary that's because it was supposed to. That's how I feel when I walk through the streets.

Overall though, I remember I had a lot of enjoyable moments on the trip. I visited New Delhi, Jaipur and Agra for the first time - and it was totally wicked. Also a lot of my close cousins were able to visit at the same time so I enjoyed spending time with them. Here are some pictures:

Celebration Night
Spring 2012/13
Business Celebration Night is an event held by the Business Club where business folks from around the province are invited to help us celebrate our business program. The event is formal and the goal is to impress and get more adults involved in high school business. Awards are handed out to students in several categories to celebrate their individual and group achievements in business from that school year. The event is has a photo booth, refreshments, musical entertainment and speeches.

As both President and VP Events of the Business Club (read more about the Business Club below), Business Celebration Night was a very important night for me in both years that I was with the Club. The two Nights I was involved happened to be the first and second time something like this was ever done, so decisions had to be made on the theme, style of the invitations and program cards, type of refreshments, decoration of the school, and many other things. The month leading up to the night was very hectic and delegating duties became a large part of the event for me. Mosaic
Summer 2012/13
Mosaic is a festival held throughout the city of Regina organised by the Regina Multicultural Council (RMC) and celebrates the cultures of the city. Each cultural group has a pavilion where they show off entertainment, food and things of cultural significance. My family and I have been part of the Indian pavilion during Mosaic for many years, usually contributing as volunteers and entertainment. (My dance videos are below) In my final two years in Regina, I joined the India-Canada Association (ICA) as the Youth Ambassador, whose main job is to organise the entertainment at the varies events the ICA hosts - naturally Mosiac is one of the larger ones. As Youth Ambassador, I automatically take a seat on the RMC as one of the many Mosaic Youth Representatives whose main responsibility is to help publicise Mosaic as a whole.

Being Youth Ambassador was a very maddening experience. Everyone else, as adults, think they can do your job better than you, so the job became just as much dealing with egos and people as creating an entertainment schedule and MCing. But like Celebration Night, the end of each day at Mosaic are a lot more enjoyable, probably because I was on stage.

Business Club
2011-2013
In my Grade 11 year, one of my favourite teachers launched the Business Club, an extra-ciricular extension of the school's business program. I was elected VP Events that year and, along with the other executive members, went on to build the club as something that can last year to year. With the teacher-advisor's business contacts we were invited to a lot of business events from around the provice, one of which was the first annual Saskatchewn High School Business Case Competition. I was one of the members of the team from my school and together we brought home the " Business School of the Year" award!

The next year I assumed the mantle of President, where my job was to oversee and organise the club's week-to-week activity. This meant arranging and moderating the club’s weekly meetings, which included consulting with teacher advisors and planning and discussing ventures for the club. In a larger venture to try and make the Club more fluid from year to year, I wrote six “Department Packages” for each department of the club. These were to help establish roles and responsibilities of all members of the club so that each year when students graduated, there would be documentation for other students to replace them.

Naturally, Business Club's Celebration Night was also a large part of my duties in both years, more about that is above. In my final year, I was awarded "Business Student of the Year" for all my efforts to build up the club. Looking back I truely enjoyed my time with the club and I'm really glad I could be such a big part of it.
