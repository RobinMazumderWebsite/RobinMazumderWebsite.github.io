Revitalizing ENCMP

Winter 2017

Only by speaking and conversing to and with first year engineering students at the University of Alberta, can you understand how terrible the perspective of Computer Engineering is for these students. A big part of that is ENCMP 100, a course that all first year engineers take that teaches MATLAB. ENCMP teaches students code and syntax, and not problem solving or any other skill Computer Engineers use.
The lack of understanding gets me heated. Brittany and I are working with Ivan Fair to make the course better. I hope to introduce focus on problem solving, resourcefulness and debugging as soft skills. I believe that people that are taught how to code by focusing on what can be created from the skill is a really good way to grow passion in the subject, let's see if it's possible :D

HackTime

Winter 2017

HackTime is a project I've been interested in doing for slightly less than a year since beginning it. The concept is to bring together coders more consistently than hackathons allow for. With the goal is to encourage students to work on side projects, simply booking a room and coding together can create a safe space and community for these students.
When the new VP Social for CompE Club had a similar idea, I jumped on it. Unfortunately, the project is taking longer to get off the ground than most of my projects. I don't want to be too involved to give more time to other projects, and others seem to be too busy. Hopefully it'll get going next year :D

CE approval of AU Courses

Fall 2016

I was disappointed in courses I could take for electives, but I heard from a friend that Computer Engineers at the University of Alberta could take electives online through Athabasca University. Out of all the courses approved for credits by the Director of Computer Engineering, only one of them stuck out. Unfortunately it was no longer offered by AU. I looked through AU courses offered for good ones I'd like to take and decided to approach the Director about them. Understanding that I brought him courses the University did not offer already, he approved them for me and added them to the list of approved courses from AU.

Since getting those courses approved, I've learned that a lot of my peers are joining me in taking them. :D I was able to expand the scope of my degree for myself and for others.

How to CompE workshops

Fall 2016

Once former president, Brittany Lamorie and I found that being executive members of the club and trying to create new opportunities for CompE's was very difficult, we decided that we would not be a part of CompE Club and instead work on events like these. Read my emotionally charged blog post on it here, but essentially How to CompE is a series of 4 workshops (Networking, Job Search, Resume and Interview) to help CompE's be better prepared navigating the industry.
The events went really well, but unfortunately the timing was bad and turnout was underwhelming. We're taking steps to fix this to bring the good teachings to more students.
