CE approval of AU Courses

Fall 2016

I was disappointed in courses I could take for electives, but I heard from a friend that Computer Engineers at the University of Alberta could take electives online through Athabasca University. Out of all the courses approved for credits by the Director of Computer Engineering, only one of them stuck out. Unfortunately it was no longer offered by AU. I looked through AU courses offered for good ones I'd like to take and decided to approach the Director about them. Understanding that I brought him courses the University did not offer already, he approved them for me and added them to the list of approved courses from AU.

Since getting those courses approved, I've learned that a lot of my peers are joining me in taking them. :D I was able to expand the scope of my degree for myself and for others.

How to CompE workshops

Fall 2016

Once former president, Brittany Lamorie and I found that being executive members of the club and trying to create new opportunities for CompE's was very difficult, we decided that we would not be a part of CompE Club and instead work on events like these. Read my emotionally charged blog post on it here, but essentially How to CompE is a series of 4 workshops (Networking, Job Search, Resume and Interview) to help CompE's be better prepared navigating the industry.
The events went really well, but unfortunately the timing was bad and turnout was underwhelming. We're taking steps to fix this to bring the good teachings to more students.

ECE Week, HackEd 2016

Fall 2015

As VP External for the Computer Engineering (CompE) Club, my task was to organize and run ECE Week and our Hackathon, HackEd. This requred creating websites, creating a professional sponsorship package, contacting over 100 different companies, interacting with industry professionals, and MC-ing the hackathon's opening ceremonies. Contacting companies was ecspecially challenging as I led all the CompE Club members to help me. I wrote an email outline and listed companies for members to find contact info for and contact. Overall ECE Week and the Hackathon were successful events!
